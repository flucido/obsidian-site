# NeroDesign Differentiation Claims

## What Makes Us Distinct

### 1. Evidence-Backed Design Decisions
**Claim:** Every interface choice traces to neuroaesthetic research.
**Proof:** Our Evidence Ledger classifies every design rule by evidence tier (Strong Evidence, Interpretive Framework, Studio Methodology). Clients can see the research behind every decision.
**Contrast:** Most agencies justify decisions with "best practices," "what converts," or "what looks good."

### 2. Emotional Specifications
**Claim:** Every page has a target emotional coordinate (PAD values), not just a visual mockup.
**Proof:** Our page archetypes define target Pleasure, Arousal, and Dominance values, and all design tokens are calibrated to match.
**Contrast:** Most agencies design for visual appeal and conversion metrics, not emotional states.

### 3. Revisability by Default
**Claim:** Every interaction supports undo, draft, preview, or return.
**Proof:** Our component patterns bake revisability into forms, CTAs, and navigation. It is not an afterthought.
**Contrast:** Most agencies treat reversibility as an edge case, not a core requirement.

### 4. Critique Heuristics
**Claim:** Every design passes six evidence-based gates before delivery.
**Proof:** Our Critique Heuristics framework evaluates element count, arousal calibration, revisability, dominance, implicit aesthetic, and accessibility.
**Contrast:** Most agencies rely on subjective review or client approval, not systematic evaluation.

### 5. Cognitive Load Management
**Claim:** We design for users with reduced cognitive energy, not peak performers.
**Proof:** Our 9-17 element constraint, generous spacing, and processing fluency rules ensure interfaces work for users at their most vulnerable.
**Contrast:** Most agencies design for the "average" user, who does not exist in high-sensitivity contexts.

## What We Are Not

- We are not a generalist agency. We serve high-sensitivity sectors.
- We do not optimize for vanity metrics (page views, time on site) over user well-being.
- We do not use urgency, scarcity, or manipulative patterns.
- We do not design from trends. We design from evidence.
