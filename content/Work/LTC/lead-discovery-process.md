# LTC Lead Discovery Process

## Purpose
Identify California school districts with data infrastructure needs that match Lucido Technology Consulting's services: CALPADS compliance, SIS migration/optimization, ETL data pipelines, FERPA compliance, data governance, and data quality consulting.

---

## Phase 1: District Qualification Criteria

A qualified LTC prospect meets **3+** of these signals:

### Tier 1 Signals (High Intent)
- [ ] **Active SIS RFP** — District is actively procuring or migrating a Student Information System
- [ ] **CALPADS Differentiated Assistance** — District flagged by CDE for data submission failures or data quality issues
- [ ] **Public data quality findings** — Audit reports, board minutes referencing CALPADS errors, data governance gaps
- [ ] **SIS contract expiration** — Known SIS contract ending within 12-18 months

### Tier 2 Signals (Medium Intent)
- [ ] **Large or complex district** — 5,000+ ADA, multiple school types, high FRL/EL populations
- [ ] **Recent leadership change** — New Superintendent, CTO, or Data Officer in last 18 months
- [ ] **Technology modernization initiative** — Board-adopted tech plan, bond-funded IT upgrades
- [ ] **Regional COE data support dependency** — District relies on COE for data services they'd prefer to own

### Tier 3 Signals (Awareness)
- [ ] **FERPA-sensitive programs** — Special education, foster youth, homeless youth, mental health services
- [ ] **Multiple data systems** — Using 3+ different platforms that require integration
- [ ] **Pending accountability actions** — Dashboard indicators showing need for improvement
- [ ] **Nearby district success** — Neighboring district has invested in data infrastructure

## Phase 2: Discovery Sources

### Primary Sources
1. **CDE CALPADS Communications** — https://www.cde.ca.gov/ds/sp/cl/ — Back-to-school letters, DA eligibility lists, submission deadlines
2. **District RFP Portals** — Individual district purchasing/contracts pages
3. **County Office of Education** — Regional data support services, needs assessments
4. **California School Dashboard** — https://www.caschooldashboard.org/ — Identify underperforming districts
5. **EdWeek / EdSource** — California education technology coverage

### Secondary Sources
6. **District board meeting minutes** — Technology updates, data governance discussions
7. **LCAP (Local Control Accountability Plan)** — Districts must publish goals; data infrastructure often mentioned
8. **Ed-Data.org** — District demographic and financial profiles
9. **CSIS (California School Information Services)** — Technical support provider for many districts
10. **LinkedIn** — CTO/Data Officer job postings signal growing districts

## Phase 3: Verification Gates (LTC-Adapted)

| Gate | Check | Method |
|------|-------|--------|
| **G1: District exists** | CDE directory lookup | https://www.cde.ca.gov/ds/si/ds/pubschls.asp |
| **G2: Need confirmed** | At least 1 Tier 1 or 2 Tier 2 signals documented | Public RFP, board minutes, CDE reports |
| **G3: Decision-maker identified** | Superintendent, CTO, or Data Officer found | District org chart, LinkedIn, board minutes |
| **G4: Budget signal** | LCAP shows funding, or bond measure, or RFP budget allocated | LCAP document, board-approved budget |
| **G5: Timeline** | Active procurement cycle or known planning window | RFP dates, board calendar, fiscal year |

---

## Phase 4: Outreach Approach

- **Warm referral preferred** — COE connections, CSIS partners, existing district relationships
- **Trigger-based outreach** — RFP response, post-audit follow-up, post-Dashboard release
- **Deliverable-first** — Offer a CALPADS readiness assessment or data quality audit as initial touch
- **FERPA-aware** — All outreach must acknowledge student data privacy requirements upfront
