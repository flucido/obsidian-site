# What Building School IT Infrastructure Taught Me

---

The biggest problem in school IT is usually not the hardware. It is the gap between what districts are responsible for and what their systems actually make possible.

School IT teams are expected to hold together an ecosystem that includes:

• student information systems
• assessment platforms
• identity and access management
• state reporting workflows
• network reliability
• privacy and compliance requirements
• support for students, staff, and families

All at once.
Usually without extra margin in the budget, the timeline, or the staffing model.

After working in and around this space, one lesson keeps repeating:

The districts that build resilience are not always the ones with the biggest budgets.
They are the ones that make better infrastructure choices early.

That usually means:

• preferring systems with open APIs
• demanding exportable data in standard formats
• treating interoperability as a requirement, not a feature request
• building internal knowledge instead of outsourcing every critical function

Open formats, Ed-Fi, CEDS, documented integrations, reproducible reporting, clean identity architecture.

These are not abstract technical preferences.
They are the difference between an IT environment that compounds capability and one that compounds friction.
