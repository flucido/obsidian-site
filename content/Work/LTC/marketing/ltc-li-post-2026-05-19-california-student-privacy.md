# FERPA Is Not the Only Student Privacy Law California Districts Need to Worry About

**Hook:** FERPA is not the only student privacy law California districts need to worry about. It is only one part of a larger federal-and-state privacy framework.

---

FERPA is not the only student privacy law California districts need to worry about. It is only one part of a larger federal-and-state privacy framework.

That matters because a lot of vendors still position "FERPA-compliant" as if it closes the conversation.

It does not.

The California Department of Education's Student Records & Privacy page makes this clear. CDE points districts to both federal and California privacy laws, not FERPA alone:

• FERPA for education records
• PPRA for surveys and parent rights
• CIPA for internet safety requirements tied to E-rate funding
• COPPA for online services used by children under 13
• SOPIPA for K-12 student online personal information
• AB 1584 for third-party contracts involving student data
• AB 1442 for social media monitoring requirements
• CCPA and CPRA where broader personal data practices are involved
• California Education Code and state reporting obligations

The risk is not just legal.
It is operational.

If your district only audits vendors against FERPA, you can still miss major gaps around targeted advertising, profiling, data-sharing restrictions, parent notice processes, internet safety requirements, and auditability.

The most practical starting point I recommend:

1. Review vendors against the full California stack, not just FERPA.
2. Classify what student data each platform touches.
3. Confirm you can produce access logs and disclosure records on demand.
4. Document how parent requests are actually handled.

Compliance is not a badge.
It is a system.
