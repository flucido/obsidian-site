## Pitch Strategy

**Core angle:** SCCOE launched CARE (CALPADS Rules Engine) in April 2026 — a tool that lets districts check their CALPADS data against the same validation rules the state uses, but earlier and with clearer feedback. The tool finds the problems. The question is whether districts — especially the ones in fiscal crisis — have the capacity to fix them. LTC positions as CARE implementation partner: extending the tool's value from detection to resolution, through a COE-level shared services model that serves all 46 DataZone districts.

**Key tension:** Three DataZone member districts (Alum Rock, Berryessa, Franklin-McKinley) are in active fiscal crisis — cutting staff, closing schools. DataZone membership and CARE tool access don't help if there's no one left to act on the findings. The COE's investment in CARE creates demand for implementation support that districts' own budgets can't absorb. LTC fills that gap.

**Specific to Shahin:** He's the Director of Integrated Data — CARE and DataZone are his domain. The April 2026 CARE launch is his team's accomplishment. Frame LTC as complementing and extending that work, not competing with it. The pitch isn't "your tool needs help" — it's "your tool creates an opportunity to deliver more value to districts, and we can be the capacity that makes that happen."

**Differentiation:** Not proposing a replacement for CARE or DataZone. Not pitching a retainer. Offering to be the implementation layer — helping districts resolve the validation errors CARE surfaces — through a COE service agreement model that can be funded through existing shared services budgets or district pass-through. One partnership = support across 46 districts.

**CTA:** Brief conversation about how LTC could complement CARE's rollout for member districts. Peer-to-peer tone — two data professionals discussing implementation capacity.
