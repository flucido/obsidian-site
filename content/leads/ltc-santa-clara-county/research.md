# Santa Clara County — LTC Opportunity Research

## Executive Summary

Santa Clara County Office of Education (SCCOE) operates **DataZone**, a student data warehouse serving **46 districts across 3 counties** with 300,000+ students. In April 2026, SCCOE launched **CARE** (CALPADS Rules Engine) — a proactive data quality validation tool built into DataZone. This creates an immediate LTC opportunity at two levels:

1. **COE-level:** Partner with SCCOE DataZone as a technical implementation partner for CARE adoption and district onboarding
2. **District-level:** Target distressed districts that are cutting staff but must maintain CALPADS compliance

The county has 3 districts in active fiscal crisis — all DataZone members — creating urgency around data quality during staff reductions.

### Overview

- **Scale:** 46 districts across 3 counties, 300,000+ students

### CARE Tool Details
- Built into existing DataZone platform — no separate uploads
- Uses same validation rules as CDE, but presented in clearer/actionable format
- Designed for cross-team collaboration (student services, data, IT, program staff)
- Purpose: catch CALPADS errors BEFORE state submission
- Launched April 2026 — this is the onboarding window

### LTC Pitch to SCCOE
"Your CARE tool catches errors, but districts still need to fix them. When a district has 10,000+ CALPADS validation flags and a data team that just got cut in half, the tool identifies problems faster than they can solve them. LTC can be the implementation arm — we take the CARE output and help districts resolve the data quality issues before submission deadlines."

### Why SCCOE Would Care
- CARE adoption is only valuable if districts can act on the results
- Distressed districts are cutting the very staff who handle data quality
- LTC can be positioned as a shared service through DataZone, not a separate vendor
- SCCOE already uses a "shared services model" — LTC fits that narrative
