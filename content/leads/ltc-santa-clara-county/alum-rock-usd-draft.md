## Pitch Strategy

**Core angle:** Alum Rock completed a Baseline Systems Study in Summer 2025 — a comprehensive review of the systems influencing student outcomes. They're a DataZone member with access to SCCOE's new CARE (CALPADS Rules Engine) tool. They have the diagnostics. But after cutting 38 positions in February and facing an $8M deficit, the question is whether the remaining staff can act on what those diagnostics reveal. LTC offers a targeted, project-based engagement to turn DataZone insights and CARE findings into CALPADS compliance — without adding headcount the budget can't support.

**Key tension:** The CTO is intact and forward-looking (Littrell survived the layoffs, still has a vision). But the institutional knowledge about data processes may have walked out the door with those 38 positions. SCCOE is watching — Alum Rock is under county oversight from past mismanagement. A CALPADS certification failure under oversight isn't just embarrassing; it's a visible metric that erodes trust with the county. The cost of a failed cycle dwarfs a short remediation engagement.

**Specific to Littrell:** His own ITSS vision statement emphasizes TCO/ROI models, automation, security, and "making optimal use of available resources." He thinks like us — efficient, well-designed systems, not vendor bloat. Frame LTC as aligned with that philosophy: a focused engagement that documents workflows, trains remaining staff, and builds validation rules that catch errors before submittal. Not a retainer. Not a software sale. A project with a finish line.

**Differentiation:** Not pitching new tools. Alum Rock already has DataZone and CARE. LTC helps them actually use what they've already invested in. The Baseline Systems Study may have flagged data infrastructure gaps — if so, LTC addresses them. If not, LTC ensures the study's recommendations don't sit on a shelf. Time-boxed to 2-3 months. Could be grant-funded (Title I, LCFF supplemental).

**Budget sensitivity:** Explicitly not FTE. Not a recurring line item. Project-based with defined deliverables and an end date. Positioned as filling a gap created by the layoffs, not as new spending. The Baseline Systems Study and CARE access are already paid for — LTC just makes those investments pay off.

**CTA:** Short conversation — peer-to-peer between two people who care about well-designed systems. Not a proposal. A discussion about where the data team needs support after a hard year.
