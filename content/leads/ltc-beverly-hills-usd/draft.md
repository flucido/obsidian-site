## Pitch Strategy

**Core angle:** BHUSD's 2025-26 LCAP includes a "Data Management: Implementing software" initiative with projected funding. The question is whether this refers to data infrastructure (SIS, CALPADS, reporting systems) or instructional software (learning platforms, assessment tools). This outreach is exploratory — opening a conversation to understand the scope, not pitching a solution.

**Key tension:** BHUSD is a small district (~3,200 students) — likely lacks dedicated data engineering staff. If the LCAP initiative is infrastructure-focused, there's a gap between the software purchase and the implementation expertise needed to make it work. If it's instructional software, this lead is probably not an LTC fit.

**Differentiation:** This is a genuine inquiry, not a disguised pitch. The email acknowledges uncertainty about scope and asks for clarification. Low-pressure. Designed to open a door or get a clean "wrong person" redirect — either outcome is useful at this stage of qualification.

**CTA:** Brief conversation — "help me understand what you're building." No proposal language. No budget discussion. Pure discovery.
